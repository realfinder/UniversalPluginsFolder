Examples

Quality Improvement Examples:

The following examples show 3 images side by side.  The left and middle are 2 images that are being compared.  The right most image is the “range expanded” difference between the left and center images.  They are all from an old “1995” very badly encoded TV show.  Some of the encoding errors include having a group of blocks shifted out of their correct position by 1 or 2 pixels, reducing the height by ½ by removing the lines vs. doing a proper resize, and using way too few bits on the encode.  The encoded frame size is352x240. 

 

I have stripped the color using greyscale() since SmoothD2 only operates on the luma and it is easier to see the changes in in black and white.  After each image is processed by SmoothD2, pointresize() is used to double the image size so the differences are easier to see.

 

The calls to create the left and center images are given directly below the examples.

Quality Improvements:  The biggest quality improvement, which is shown in the following examples, is the ability to deblock more strongly while at the same time retaining more detail. Some of this is from the improved shift resampling positions. Most comes from the ability to use Zero Weight, specifically ZWlmDark and ZWlmBright, to vary the deblocking strength on a per pixel bases, controlled by the luma value and the location within a macroblock.
