Strength vs. Detail in SmoothD2() and SmoothD()

In the 2 examples that follow the SmoothD2() arguments stay the same and the SmoothD() quant argument is changed.  In SmoothD2()  num_shift=2 does 8 shifts so it is the equivalent to num_shift=8 in SmoothD2().

In the first example, to have the equivalent coat texture as SmoothD2(), SmoothD() needs to use quant=10.  This leaves a lot of noise in the white areas, most notably in the left forehead region.  It also leaves some blocking in the mouth and nose area, as well as in the hair.

Left   SmoothD2(quant=18, num_shift=2, Matrix=8, Qtype=1, ZW=0, ZWce=1, ZWlmDark=255, ZWlmBright=140)

Center SmoothD(quant=10, num_shift=8)   # Match coat texture detail.

 

 

In the second example to have the equivalent deblocking in the face as SmoothD2(), SmoothD() needs quant=26.  This leaves the coat over smoothed and the hair having lost some detail. 


Left   SmoothD2(quant=18, num_shift=2, Matrix=8, Qtype=1, ZW=0, ZWce=1, ZWlmDark=255, ZWlmBright=140)

Center SmoothD(quant=26, num_shift=8)   # Match face deblocking.
